About PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It is characterized by an imbalance of reproductive hormones, leading to various symptoms such as irregular menstrual cycles, excessive hair growth, acne, and difficulty in conceiving.

PCOS can cause enlarged ovaries with small cysts on the outer edges. It is also associated with insulin resistance and an increased risk of other health problems such as type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and endometrial cancer.

Common Symptoms of PCOS

  • Irregular or absent menstrual cycles
  • Excessive hair growth (hirsutism)
  • Acne or oily skin
  • Male-pattern baldness or thinning hair
  • Difficulty in conceiving (infertility)

Diagnosis and Treatment

PCOS is typically diagnosed based on symptoms, blood tests to check hormone levels, and ultrasound imaging of the ovaries. While there is no cure for PCOS, treatments such as medication, lifestyle changes, and hormonal therapies can help manage symptoms and reduce the risk of long-term health problems.

With early detection and proper management, individuals with PCOS can lead a healthy and fulfilling life.
